WAYANAD


Wayanad is one of the most popular tourist destinations of Kerala. It is known for its pleasant climate throughout the year, and for its scenic beauty. Its proximity to places like Bangalore, Mysore, Chennai and Kochi makes it a favourite destination among travellers in Karnataka, Tamil Nadu and Kerala. Only District Sharing Borders With Karnataka and Tamil Nadu

POPULATION : 8.17 lakhs

AREA : 2132 km2

TOURIST SPOTS

EDAKKAL CAVE

Edakkal caves are well known for its pictorial depictions (cave art), which are thought to be of 6000 B C. To achieve the hollows, one needs to trek through the Ambukutty Mala. It will take around 45 minutes to climb the hill and you will never get baffled in your outing to these historical caves & it is one of the famous places to see in Wayanad.

SOOCHIPARA WATERFALLS

Soochipara waterfall is a three-layered waterfall at Vellarimala in Wayanad. This waterfall, encompassed by thick green woodland, is otherwise called Sentinel Rock Waterfall. A 20 minute drive from Meppadi in Wayanad will take you to this awe inspiring waterfall & it is one of the best Wayanad places to see. If you are looking for some fun-filled Wayanad tourist places then the famous Neelima View Point is a must visit for you.

MONUMENTS

Jain temple : People in Kerala believe that Jains were the first ones who came to the district of Wayanad. Maybe this is the reason Wayanad is home to many Jain temples. One among such temples is the Jain temple at Panamaram. History states that the Jains first established their dwelling place near the banks of Kabani River in Panamaram. This became their starting point. After that, the Jains spread all over Wayanad and thereafter, in the whole of Kerala.

Pazhassi Memorial : ​ Mananthavady is the spot of this memorial, which is dedicated to Veera Pazhassi, the ’Lion of Kerala,’ who valiantly fought a guerrilla war against the British. It is located at a scenic spot by the Kabani River. Make sure to take your kids to the Pulpally Cave, which house a collection of his belongings.
